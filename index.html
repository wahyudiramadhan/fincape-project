About the Project

Flood Impacts,
Carbon Pricing &
Ecosystem Sustainability

FINCAPES is a comprehensive multi-stakeholder initiative that addresses the intersection of climate finance, flood risk management, and biodiversity conservation. Working across Jambi, Kalimantan, and Lampung, we build evidence-based strategies for sustainable ecosystem finance.

Flood Impact Assessment

Mapping flood vulnerability and building community resilience across river basins

Carbon Pricing Mechanisms

Developing REDD+ and voluntary carbon market instruments for forest protection

Ecosystem Sustainability

Protecting peatlands, tropical forests, and biodiversity corridors

Community Partnership

Co-designing conservation solutions with indigenous and local communities

Jambi Kalimantan Lampung Univ. of Waterloo
FINCAPES Focus Areas

Three Thematic Pillars

Coordinated action across flood risk governance, nature-based solutions, and climate finance to build lasting ecosystem resilience in Indonesia.

Flood Risk Governance

Strengthening multi-level governance frameworks and institutional capacity for flood risk management across Jambi, East Kalimantan, and Lampung river basins.

  • Flood vulnerability mapping & early warning systems
  • River basin assessment across 38 watersheds
  • Community-level flood resilience plans
  • Policy briefs on integrated water resource management
Knowledge Products View on Map

Nature-based Solutions

Implementing gender-responsive peatland and mangrove restoration programs that integrate biodiversity conservation with community livelihoods across three provinces.

  • Peatland restoration across 180,000+ hectares
  • Mangrove rehabilitation in coastal Lampung
  • Gender-inclusive community conservation co-design
  • Biodiversity corridor management plans
Knowledge Products I-CAN Platform

Climate Finance & Policy

Developing REDD+, voluntary carbon market, and green finance instruments to mobilize sustainable funding for ecosystem conservation and climate policy commitments.

  • REDD+ carbon credit frameworks (1.4 Mt CO₂ verified)
  • Green bond & blended finance instruments
  • Policy dialogue with KLHK & sub-national governments
  • Capacity-building for climate finance institutions
